## 2) The interactive Life Vision Experience (digital-first, printable output)

### What users do on the platform

They complete the worksheets as guided modules with:

* short questions (not big blank pages),
* sliders, tick-boxes, and journaling fields,
* auto-summaries that make them feel “seen” and supported.

### What the platform generates (download/print)

At any time they can export:

* **My Life Vision Booklet (PDF)** – their full completed workbook
* **My 90-Day Plan (PDF)** – outcomes, weekly anchors, milestones
* **My Habit Tracker (PDF)** – month view aligned to chosen habits
* **My Vision Board Page (PDF)** – images/words arranged cleanly

This matches the aesthetic you shared: users do the work digitally, then get a beautiful artefact they can keep.

## 3) The “keep coming back” engine (without feeling like an app that nags)

Retention comes from a gentle rhythm, not pressure.

### Daily (2 minutes): “Today’s Faithful Step”

* Tick habits (1–3 max)
* Quick mood/energy check (1–5)
* One short prompt: “What would make today meaningful?”
* Optional: prayer note (“Lord, help me with…”)

### Weekly (10 minutes): “Weekly Review + Reset”

* One win (celebrate progress)
* One lesson (growth mindset)
* One adjustment (simplify)
* Choose next week’s top 3 priorities
* Optional: “Where did I notice God’s help this week?”

### Monthly (15 minutes): “Life Balance Refresh”

* Re-score Wheel of Life
* Spot patterns and drift
* Reset one habit or boundary
* Export updated plan if desired

### Quarterly (30–45 minutes): “New Season Planning”

* Review outcomes
* Refresh vision + 90-day sprint
* Update vision board
* Write a fresh future-self note

This becomes a healthy spiritual/practical routine—something people *want* to return to.

## 4) Community + ministry layer (supportive, not salesy)

This is where reawakened.one becomes more than worksheets.

### Community features that feel like discipleship

* **Prayer Wall (optional, with privacy controls)**
  Users can keep requests private, share with community, or share with a small group.
* **Accountability Pairing (“Vision Partners”)**
  A gentle opt-in: “Would you like someone to walk with you?”
* **Small Groups (“Growth Circles”)**
  6–10 people, simple weekly prompts aligned to the Journey.
* **Encouragement Threads**
  “What helped you stay consistent this week?”
* **Testimony Moments**
  A guided reflection: “What has changed in me since I started?”

### Ministry-safe design note

Make community participation **opt-in**, with clear boundaries, reporting tools, and gentle moderation. People share more when it feels safe and honoured.
