## Part 4: Revival Meeting Formats

### Format A: "The Welsh Model" - Spirit-Led Gathering
*Based on 1904-1905 Welsh Revival characteristics*

**Structure:**
- No rigid program or lengthy sermons
- Heavy emphasis on spontaneous singing
- Space for public testimony and confession
- Prayer erupts organically
- Meetings often extend for hours

**Practical Implementation:**
```
Opening (15 min): Worship songs, creating atmosphere of expectation
Testimony Time (20-30 min): 3-4 prepared + spontaneous testimonies
"The Hush" (variable): Extended worship, waiting on the Spirit
Confession/Response (variable): Space for Holy Spirit conviction
Prayer Eruption (variable): Corporate, simultaneous, passionate prayer
Closing Song: Sending song with commissioning prayer
```

**Key Elements:**
- No time limits announced
- Worship team prepared with extensive song list
- Microphones available for testimonies
- Ministry team ready for prayer/counsel
- Communion elements available

---

### Format B: "The Pyongyang Model" - Bible Study Revival
*Based on 1907 Korean Revival*

**Structure:**
- Intensive Bible study (conference format)
- Building toward corporate response
- Confession and reconciliation central
- Dawn prayer component

**Practical Implementation:**
```
PRE-CONFERENCE:
- 4+ weeks of noon prayer meetings
- Distribute Bible study materials in advance

CONFERENCE FORMAT (3-5 days):
Morning (6-7am): Dawn prayer meeting
Morning Session (9am-12pm): Intensive Bible exposition
Afternoon (2-5pm): Small group discussion, personal application
Evening (7-10pm): Corporate gathering, testimonies, altar response

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENTS:
- Simultaneous audible prayer (Korean style)
- Public confession opportunity
- Reconciliation time (with specific individuals)
- Extended altar ministry
```

---

### Format C: "The Businessmen's Model" - Workplace Revival
*Based on 1857 New York Revival*

**Structure:**
- Noon prayer meetings (lunch hour)
- Brief, focused, accessible
- Professional setting friendly
- Multiplication-oriented

**Practical Implementation:**
```
Weekly Noon Prayer (45 min):
- 5 min: Opening song/Scripture
- 5 min: Brief devotional thought
- 30 min: Prayer requests and intercession
- 5 min: Closing, announcements

Quarterly Extended Gathering (90 min):
- Testimony sharing
- Deeper teaching
- Extended prayer
- Vision casting
```

**Multiplication Strategy:**
Start with 6 people (like Lanphier's first meeting)
Grow to 20, then 40
Split and multiply to multiple locations
Goal: Prayer in every workplace building

---

### Format D: "The Campus Model" - Student Revival
*Based on Mount Hermon (1886), Asbury (1970, 2023)*

**Structure:**
- Extended worship/prayer (can last days)
- No scheduled end time
- Student-led, faculty-supported
- Spontaneous spread to other campuses

**Practical Implementation:**
```
GATHERING LAUNCH:
- Begin with normal chapel/meeting
- Create space for Holy Spirit to move
- Don't dismiss when presence is strong
- Rotate worship/prayer leadership

SUSTAINING ELEMENTS:
- 24/7 prayer room established
- Meals brought in
- Faculty cover classes as needed
- Livestream for those who can't attend physically
- Send teams to other campuses

DOCUMENTATION:
- Record testimonies
- Capture video for wider distribution
- Document changed lives, reconciled relationships
```

## Part 7: Prayer Mobilization Framework

### "The Moravian Model" - Sustained Prayer Networks

**Historical Foundation:**
> The Moravians established a "24/7" prayer watch that lasted over 100 years.

**Modern Implementation:**

1. **Regional Prayer Chains**
   - 24-hour coverage across time zones
   - 1-hour slots, weekly commitment
   - Shared prayer focus each week
   - Digital sign-up and reminders

2. **Noon Prayer Movement**
   - Based on Pyongyang model
   - Daily 12:00pm (local time)
   - Even 5 minutes of focused prayer
   - United theme across network

3. **Campus Prayer Watches**
   - Student-led, 24/7 rooms
   - Following CICCU/Mount Hermon model
   - Prayer for campus, city, nation
   - Special extended times during exams/stress

### "The Khasi Hills Pattern" - Building Toward Breakthrough

**Historical Timeline:**
```
1903: Monday night prayer meetings begin
1904: News of Welsh Revival intensifies prayer
Early 1905: Meetings expand to EVERY evening
18+ months: Sustained prayer continues
March 1905: REVIVAL BREAKS OUT
```

**Modern Application:**
- Begin with weekly prayer gatherings
- Increase frequency as hunger grows
